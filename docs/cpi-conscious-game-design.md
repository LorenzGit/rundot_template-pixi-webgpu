# CPI-conscious game design

Last reviewed: 2026-07-22.

Cost per install (CPI) is an acquisition metric, not a design score:

```text
CPI = eligible campaign spend ÷ attributed installs
```

Category matters because it changes audience size, auction competition,
expectations, monetization, and likely lifetime value. But the immediate CPI of
a campaign can be influenced more strongly by its first seconds, fantasy,
visual readability, theme, and creative execution. Treat that ordering as a
testable hypothesis, never a universal law.

## Category sets the market; creative earns the attention

Broad casual categories such as puzzle, runner, idle, and simulation may have a
larger addressable audience. Strategy, RPG, sports, 4X, or other specialized
categories may compete for narrower players with different LTV expectations.
Neither category is inherently better: a higher CPI can be rational when
retention and value are correspondingly higher.

The same mechanic can produce materially different acquisition results when
framed as:

- abstract science-fiction resource management;
- cozy village building;
- zombie-survival base building.

The fantasy changes who stops, who understands the goal, and who expects the
game behind the ad. It does not change the underlying loop’s quality.

## A practical creative hierarchy to test

1. **First 1–3 seconds:** Is the action, conflict, transformation, or choice
   immediately legible?
2. **Theme and fantasy:** Does the viewer understand who they are and what they
   get to do?
3. **Art direction and readability:** Is the focal action clear at phone size?
4. **Genre/category promise:** Does the creative attract people who want the
   actual session structure?
5. **Gameplay depth:** Does the installed experience reward the promise over
   subsequent sessions?

Google describes creative assets as the “face” of an App campaign and
recommends varied, high-quality assets. Use that as a reason to test real
creative variants—not as proof that art can rescue a weak or misleading game.

## Design for ad legibility without building an ad fake

Build concepts with at least one honest, repeatable visual sentence:

- organize a chaotic space;
- rescue a character through a meaningful choice;
- destroy, merge, decorate, collect, reveal, upgrade, or survive;
- create a before/after transformation the actual game delivers;
- show a readable problem, player action, and consequence.

Then make the first playable session deliver that promise quickly. A cheap
install from a misleading fantasy can produce weak FTUE completion, retention,
ratings, payer conversion, and ROAS. The creative must represent real gameplay
or a truthful product fantasy supported by the game.

## Concept-test matrix

Before building a content-heavy game, test a small matrix:

| Axis | Example variants |
| --- | --- |
| Mechanic | keep one core interaction constant |
| Fantasy | cozy restoration, survival, mystery, collection |
| Art direction | graphic, painterly, realistic, stylized 3D |
| Hook | transformation, mastery, humor, suspense, expression |
| Creative format | square, portrait, landscape, short video |

Keep audience, placement, attribution, budget, and campaign goal comparable.
Change one major hypothesis at a time where practical. Test multiple executions
of each concept so one weak asset is not mistaken for a weak theme.

## Measure the complete acquisition-to-value funnel

| Layer | Metrics |
| --- | --- |
| Creative | qualified view/hold rate, click-through rate, asset rating |
| Store/landing | view-to-install conversion, attributed installs, CPI |
| FTUE | load success, first action, first value, tutorial completion |
| Product | D1/D7/D30 retention, sessions, progression, ratings |
| Business | payer conversion, ad value, ARPDAU, LTV, ROAS |
| Trust | creative-to-game mismatch feedback, early uninstall, refunds |

Never select a concept from CPI alone. Prefer the cohort with the strongest
credible path to retained value and sustainable payback. State attribution
window, platform, country, campaign goal, spend, sample size, and uncertainty
with every result.

## Theme testing checklist

- The core action reads without explanatory copy.
- The fantasy remains recognizable in a 1:1 crop and a phone-sized feed.
- The first session contains the advertised action or a truthful bridge to it.
- Store screenshots and copy match the same product promise.
- The theme can sustain months of content without incoherent reskins.
- Production cost and asset cadence fit the likely LTV.
- The concept avoids stereotypes, exploitative fear, false failure, and
  impossible choices created only for the ad.
- A high-CPI category is rejected only after multiple competent creatives and
  downstream cohorts are compared.

## RUN campaign workflow

RUN’s beta marketing flow is:

```text
prepare → references/briefs → generate → review/regenerate → preview
→ explicit spend approval → submit → status/stats
```

Use `rundot marketing preview` before any submission. Submission starts real
spend; confirm the exact game, campaign, platforms, optimization goal, and
budget first. Do not optimize toward purchase/value before trustworthy purchase
events exist.
